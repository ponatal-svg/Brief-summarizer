# ‘Something Big Is Happening’ + A.I. Rocks the Romance Novel Industry + One Good Thing

## The Hook

This episode of Hard Fork delves into the escalating societal anxiety surrounding artificial intelligence, revealing its profound impact from the halls of power in Washington D.C. to the creative industries and the stock market. It highlights a critical juncture where AI is not just enhancing existing systems but fundamentally disrupting established business models and creative processes, prompting urgent questions about the future of work and human creativity.

## Key Findings

*   **AI Panic in D.C. and Beyond**: Kevin observed "freaking out" about AI in Washington D.C., driven by increased awareness of AI's "agentic coding capabilities," its impact on tech stock valuations, and a general "ambient cultural vibe shift." People are asking if AI is happening and if their jobs are at risk.
*   **The "SASpocalypse" and Business Model Disruption**: The tech industry is experiencing a significant "sell-off" in Software as a Service (SaaS) stocks (e.g., Salesforce, Workday), with some companies' stock prices falling more than 20%. This is attributed to the growing belief that AI tools will allow businesses to "vibe code" or generate their own software solutions internally, making expensive third-party SaaS platforms less valuable. Industries like law, finance, and compliance are seen as particularly vulnerable to new AI-powered business models.
*   **Accelerated AI Development and the "Takeoff" Phase**: A viral essay by Matt Shumer, "Something Big Is Happening," highlights AI's rapid self-improvement, exemplified by OpenAI's GPT-5.3 Codex, which was reportedly instrumental in creating itself. An executive predicted that software engineering, already 90% automated, would be "fully automated" within the year, marking the "takeoff" moment for AI. The relentless and untiring nature of AI agents is cited as a key factor in this acceleration.
*   **AI's Impact on White-Collar Work and Political Repercussions**: The hosts discuss the "worker side" of AI panic, advising individuals to familiarize themselves with AI tools and prepare for potential career disruption. They stress the need for a political conversation about AI, warning of possible "permanent unemployment" in some job categories and predicting a "furious" backlash against AI that could lead to government intervention, like Bernie Sanders' recent proposal for a moratorium on AI data centers.
*   **AI Transforms the Romance Novel Industry**: AI is dramatically reshaping the romance novel industry, with authors like Coral Heart using AI to publish over 200 romance novels in a year, making six figures. This involves a complex workflow of prompting AI tools with specific tropes, settings, and even "sexual kinks" to generate stories. While some authors embrace this, seeing themselves as "directors" rather than "authors," it has sparked controversy among writers, publishers, and readers over authenticity and the commodification of creativity, especially when AI's output can be clichés like "turgid manhood" or a repeated "ragged prayer" phrase.
*   **Spotify's "Prompted Playlists" and Google's "Perch 2.0"**: On the more positive side, Spotify has launched "prompted playlists" for premium subscribers, allowing users to generate custom playlists using natural language prompts (e.g., "songs I haven't listened to in a while," "eating fish tacos on the beach"). This feature uses a user's listening data and embodies "anti-machine drift," giving users control over algorithmic recommendations. Google's "Perch 2.0," a bio-acoustics foundation model, can classify underwater sounds (like whale songs) despite being trained on bird songs, showcasing AI's ability for "transfer learning" and its potential to advance animal studies.

## The So What?

The podcast makes it clear that AI is not a distant threat but a present force transforming various sectors. Whether it's the financial markets reacting to AI's impact on software companies, creatives grappling with automated content generation, or political figures addressing job displacement, the conversation around AI has intensified. The future will likely see a blend of technological empowerment and societal pushback, necessitating ongoing adaptation from individuals, businesses, and governments alike.
