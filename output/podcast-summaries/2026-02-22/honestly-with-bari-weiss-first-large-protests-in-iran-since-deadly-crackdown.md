## The Hook
This episode covers the first large-scale protests in Iran since last month's deadly crackdown, new global trade tariffs from the US, and a cultural clash at the Berlin Film Festival, highlighting the ongoing tension between political realities and established norms.

## Key Findings
*   University students across Iran have launched significant protests, marking the first large-scale rallies since a deadly crackdown last month, with observers noting the public's increased willingness to risk defiance.
*   President Trump announced a further increase in global tariffs to 15% on imports, following a Supreme Court ruling against earlier levies, sparking global economic uncertainty and political opposition within the US.
*   At the Berlin Film Festival, controversy erupted after the head of the jury suggested cinema should remain apolitical, a stance challenged by directors and artists who used their platforms to address political issues, including the Gaza conflict and political imprisonment in Turkey.
*   An internal debate within OpenAI on whether to report a user's suspicious activity related to gun violence to the police, ultimately opting not to act before a tragic school shooting, has raised serious questions about the company's "duty of care" and the self-regulation of tech giants.
*   The ongoing release of the Epstein files reveals a global "patriarchy in action," showcasing a male-dominated network where women are largely peripheral or commodified, with many powerful acquaintances exhibiting similar behavior.
*   Japan's J-League is experimenting with eliminating draws in football matches, opting for immediate penalty shootouts to increase entertainment and provide players with high-pressure experience ahead of international competitions.
