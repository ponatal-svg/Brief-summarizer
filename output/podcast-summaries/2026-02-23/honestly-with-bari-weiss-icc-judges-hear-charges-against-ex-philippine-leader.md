# ICC judges hear charges against ex-Philippine leader

## The Hook
International legal proceedings against former Philippine President Rodrigo Duterte for crimes against humanity, coupled with escalating humanitarian crises in South Sudan and the multifaceted impacts of the Ukraine war in Russia, underscore a global landscape grappling with justice, conflict, and societal challenges.

## Key Findings
*   The International Criminal Court (ICC) has begun a long-awaited hearing to determine if former Philippine President Rodrigo Duterte should face trial for crimes against humanity, stemming from an anti-drugs crackdown that killed thousands. Duterte did not attend, citing age and health, a claim rejected by judges. His absence has angered victims' relatives who have waited years for justice. His legal team asserts his absolute innocence, claiming politically motivated charges.
*   In South Sudan, intensified fighting between government and opposition has displaced hundreds of thousands, creating an extremely critical humanitarian situation. UN Humanitarian Chief Tom Fletcher reported 6 million hungry and 7 in 10 needing aid, observing high levels of hunger, cholera, and methodical targeting of civilians. Many are resorting to eating tree leaves due to food scarcity.
*   Australia’s Prime Minister Anthony Albanese supports removing Andrew Mountbatten-Windsor from the royal line of succession. This is significant as the UK government considers legislation but requires Commonwealth agreement. Prince Andrew also faces allegations from former government officials for charging taxpayers for massages and "unnecessary" travel expenses as a trade envoy. He has consistently denied all allegations.
*   The entertainment industry faces political and corporate battles. Netflix CEO Ted Sarandos asserted their bid for Warner Bros Discovery would foster growth, contrasting it with Paramount's rival offer. He also addressed former President Trump's call for Netflix to sack Susan Rice, stating Netflix is a business, not a political entity.
*   The Ukraine war profoundly impacts Russia. BBC Russia editor Steve Rosenberg reported from Lipetsk, noting a disconnect between official narratives and daily hardships. Citizens expressed confusion about the war’s purpose, highlighting rising prices and utility bills. While direct links to the conflict are not always made, there's a widespread feeling that life is worsening with no immediate prospect of improvement.
*   A new species of Spinosaurus, featuring a unique "scimitar-crested" skull, was discovered in the Sahara Desert by Dr. Paul Sereno's team (University of Chicago). Dating back 95 million years, this 40-foot-long predator was described as a stalking hunter of coastlines, distinct from other known species.
*   Following a racial slur uttered by Tourette Syndrome campaigner John Davidson at the BAFTA Film Awards, the BBC apologized for not editing it out. Lauren Wyatt, diagnosed with Tourette's, clarified that tics are involuntary and do not reflect personal beliefs or intent. She emphasized that punishing a Tourettic individual for a tic is unjust, akin to convicting an innocent person for someone else's crime.
