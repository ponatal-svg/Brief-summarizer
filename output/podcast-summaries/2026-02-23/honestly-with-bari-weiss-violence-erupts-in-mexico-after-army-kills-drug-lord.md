## The Hook
This episode covers breaking global events, including a major drug lord's killing in Mexico and the ensuing cartel violence, escalating anti-government protests in Iran amid nuclear negotiations, and Ukrainian President Zelensky's stark claim that World War III has already begun.

## Key Findings
*   **Mexican Drug Lord's Death Sparks Violence:** The killing of Nemesio Oseguera Cervantes, known as El Mencho, leader of the Jalisco New Generation Cartel, led to violent retaliation across at least eight Mexican states. US authorities had placed a $15 million bounty on El Mencho, and commentators critical of the Mexican President suggested pressure from the US, including threats of unilateral action, likely influenced the Mexican operation.
*   **Iranian Protests and Nuclear Tensions:** Anti-government protests are widespread across Iranian universities, with students chanting "Death to the dictator." This coincides with heightened tensions over Iran's nuclear program, as President Trump set a deadline for a deal or military action. Iran's Foreign Minister insists on a "win-win game" but says enrichment is non-negotiable.
*   **Zelensky's World War III Warning:** Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky claims Russia has already started World War III, with Kyiv containing it. He insists Ukraine will regain all occupied territories, viewing any concession of land as "abandoning" its people and weakening its position.
*   **Mar-a-Lago Intruder Shot:** A 21-year-old man, Austin T. Martin, was shot dead by US Secret Service agents after attempting to breach President Trump's Mar-a-Lago estate while carrying a shotgun and a fuel can.
*   **Cultural Highlights:** The BAFTA Awards saw "One Battle After Another" win six awards, including Best Film, and Robert Aramayo made history by winning both Best Actor and the Rising Star Award. Additionally, the remains of Saint Francis of Assisi are on public display in Italy for the first time in 800 years, attracting hundreds of thousands of pilgrims.
